My City: Grottaferrata

Grottaferrata is an Italian municipality of 20,308 inhabitants in the metropolitan city of Rome Capital in Lazio.

After obtaining administrative autonomy in 1848, squeezed between Frascati, Marino, Rocca di Papa and Morena, it is currently one of the most elegant and popular holiday and residential places of the Castelli Romani, thanks to the rapid connection with Rome ensured by the state road 511 via Anagnina, which crosses the Cryptense territory for its entire extension.

The municipality is known above all for being home to the Abbey of Santa Maria di Grottaferrata, founded in 1004 by San Nilo da Rossano and currently constituted as a territorial abbey governed by the Italian Basilian Order of Grottaferrata.
